The school gives expression to the knowledge, skills, and values worth learning in South African schools, ensuring learners acquire and apply knowledge in ways that are meaningful to their own lives ‚Äî locally and globally.

Staff

Principal

Mrs. R.Z . Mvelase

Mrs. R.Z . Mvelase is a visionary leader dedicated to academic excellence and discipline at Hoye Secondary School. With decades of teaching and leadership experience, she works tirelessly to ensure that both learners and staff reach their full potential. Her focus on holistic education prepares learners for life beyond the classroom.

Deputy Principal

Mr. S.T. Mleshi

Mr. S.T. Mleshi plays a key role in the daily management of the school, supporting the principal in decision-making and ensuring smooth academic operations. He is passionate about learner discipline, teacher development, and creating a safe, productive learning environment.

Head of Science Department

Mr. N. Jule

Mr. N. Jule leads the Science Department with enthusiasm and innovation, ensuring learners develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills. He has introduced modern teaching approaches that connect classroom learning to real-world applications, inspiring future scientists and innovators.

Head of Languages Department

Mr. F.Z. Memela

Mr. F.Z. Memela is passionate about the power of language in shaping identity and communication. As Head of the Languages Department, He ensures high standards in English and IsiZulu learning, promoting literacy, cultural appreciation, and confidence in both written and spoken expression.

Head of Geography Department

Mrs I.Z Mazibuko

Mrs I.Z Mazibuko leads the Geography Department with passion and commitment. With years of experience in both teaching and field research, he ensures that learners not only understand maps and physical processes but also develop a real awareness of their environment and the challenges facing our communities.

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) provides financial assistance to eligible students at public universities and TVET colleges in South Africa.

Who Qualifies?

  • South African citizens with household income ‚â§ R350,000 per year
  • Accepted at a public university or TVET college
  • Students with disabilities ‚â§ R600,000

What NSFAS Covers

  • Registration & tuition fees
  • Accommodation if living away from home
  • Transport (up to 40 km)
  • Living allowance, book allowance, personal care allowance

How to Apply

  1. Visit www.nsfas.org.za
  2. Create a myNSFAS account
  3. Upload documents
  4. Submit before deadline

Despite her departure, she made an unforgettable gesture of gratitude by donating a professionally designed banner to the school ‚Äî a symbolic token of her appreciation and her bond with the school community.
